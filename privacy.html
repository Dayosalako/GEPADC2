Protocol.

01. Institutional Commitment

The Gender Equality Peace and Development Centre (GEPaDC) is committed to the highest standards of data protection. Given our work with survivors of Gender-Based Violence (GBV) and conflict-affected populations, we operate under a strict "Data Minimization" and "Do No Harm" framework.

02. Data Collection & Usage

We collect personal information solely for the purpose of delivering humanitarian aid, protection services, and economic empowerment programs. This data is encrypted and stored in secure institutional databases accessible only by authorized protection officers.

03. Protection of Survivors

Information regarding survivors of violence is treated with absolute confidentiality. No identifying information is shared with third-party donors or government agencies without informed, written consent, unless required for immediate life-saving medical intervention.

04. Your Rights

Individuals have the right to request access to their data, request corrections, or ask for the permanent deletion of their records from our active field rosters at any time.
